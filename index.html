Black Griffin

A gritty, low-magic campaign

Beneath the streets of Old Dismas an ancient evil stirs. Can a band of misfit mercenaries uncover the dark conspiracy and stop it before civilisation ends.

Black Griffin is a gritty, low-magic campaign for 1st-2nd level characters. This 30 page adventure also includes rules for low-magic characters, new monsters and NPCs and new factions.

Introduction

Black Griffin is a gritty, low-magic, setting neutral campaign where the characters are members of a shady mercenary company.

This book should be accompanied by a copy of the Black Griffon Player's Guide (available for free from Grisly Eye Games) which you can give to your players. It includes the rules for making characters for this campaign, as well as pre-generated characters for fast play.

Faction Escalations

Black Griffin is a campaign of intrigue and the various factions at play don't sit idly by while the party interfere with their plans. Every so often the text of the adventure will say the party have triggered an escalation. When this happens check an escalation box for the triggered faction. You can also trigger escalations if you judge the party have...[?]

Escalations can apply a penalty to the party, such as making faction members initially hostile towards them. An escalation can also trigger a faction encounter.

Faction Encounters

When you next successfully roll for a random encounter, you can choose to run a faction encounter instead. Run through any triggered faction escalations and play an appropriate one.

Act I: The Spy Thieves

In this mission, the party are asked to find the whereabouts of Vyncent, a Griffon Company spy who was, until recently, providing valuable information.

Read the following to the party:

The Black Griffin Company is on the march. It's mission, to reinforce the royalist frontline north of the ancient city of Dismas.

A company spy, Vyncent, was sent ahead to Dismas to gather information. However, he has since gone missing. Your squadron has been sent ahead to find out what happened.

His last known whereabouts: a poorhouse in the slum, Cemetery Hill.

The party should find the poorhouse with little trouble.

1. The Poorhouse

Read the following:

You can smell the rickety, poorhouse before you see it. Rough looking beggars and tramps come and go constantly, or linger outside.

The poorhouse is actually a front for a smuggling operation in-and-out of the city. A hole in a fallen-through chimney stack leads to a hidden basement. However, the baggars won't just tell strangers about it.

Old Sam (Choleric, Suspicious). Old Sam "leads" the beggars at the poorhouse. If the players start asking questions he will be pointed out to them. If they make trouble, he comes out to investigate. He has a heavy cough caused by the black mold in his "throne room" (Intelligence (Medicine) check vs DC15 to diagnose). If they players can convince him he will reveal the smugglers den beneath the poorhouse.

Characters who spend any time watching the beggars coming and going notice on a successful Intelligence (Investigation) check vs DC 13 notice a hunchback beggar entering the poorhouse and, then leaving moments later cured of his deformity!

Characters making passive Wisdom (Perception) check vs DC 15 notice the building listing a little towards the rear of the house.

2. The Smuggler's Hideout

If the players climb down the chimney stack they arrive at the smuggler's hideout. Its pitch black but if the characters have a light source read the following:

This small, damp room stacked with boxes. Some of the crates are smashed, and bottles of spirits and other contraband spill out. A man lies dead in a pool of blood in the middle of the room

The Corpse The man is not Vyncent, but a smuggler killed by a series of knife wounds to the stomach. Searching his body uncovers a folded piece of paper wrapping 9 gp. The piece of paper is a map of the smuggling route through the sewers and offers advantage on sewer crawl checks to escape the sewers.

Box of Rats. A passive Wisdom (Perception) check vs DC 10 indicates scratching noises from one box. If the characters open it two rats spill out.

Hidden Booty. On a successful Intelligence (Investigation) check vs DC 20 uncovers a hidden of 9 lapis lazuli gems worth 10 gp each, hidden in a crate.

Two vermus spies lie in watch in the shadows.

4. Old Spit's Nest

Old Spit's nest is the domain of the vermus. Random encounters here are always 1d4 vermus.

4A. Nest Entrance

Hidden Fall

Medium Mechanical Trap (200 XP)

This section of the sewers is covered in refuse, concealing an open hole covered by weakened boards and a sackcloth blanket. The boards will give way if players step on them.

A successful DC 15 Wisdom (Perception) check discerns the shape of the blanket where it has been disturbed by vermus coming and going from the nest. A successful DC 15 Intelligence (Investigation) check is necessary to confirm that the trapped section of the floor is actually the cover of a hole.

When a creature steps on the boards, they break, causing the the intruder to spill into the 20 foot pit below, taking 2d6 bludgeoning damage and landing in area 4B.

4B. Bottom of the Pit

Climbing back up their sheer surface of the hole back to the sewers requires a DC 20 Strength (Athletics). A failed check means the character gets halfway up the wall before losing their grip and falling 10 feet (1d6 bludgeoning damage). Climbing down is easier, only required a DC 10 Strength (Athletics) check.

At the bottom of the pit is pool about 1 foot deep filled with brackish, stagnant water, Various tunnels line the bottom of the pit. From within can be heard the distant sounds of scratches and rat-like chittering.

4C. Warrens

If the party enters warrens via the tunnels in area 4A they start the Warren Crawling challenge.

Warren Crawling

Challenge (level 1-4, medium, 200 XP)

The party attempt to sneak through the narrow vermus warrens without attracting attention.

Success (3): The party manages to reach their desired destination, or a random one if they're exploring (see table below). Each time they attempt to explore the warren they gain a +1 on the random nest destination roll.

Failure: The party end attract the attention of the vermus, are swarmed and dragged to area 4F to be interrogated by Old Spit.

Dexterity (Sneak) DC 15. The party attempt to sneak through the warrens without attracting the attention of the vermus. Creatures wearing heavy armour roll with disadvantage. On a failure the party are attacked by 1d4 vermus. Players must declare single-file marching order, and the DM chooses whether characters at the rear of front of the line are attacked.

Spells. Casting sleep or charm person grants an automatic success as you enchant your way past vermus sentries.

Act 2: Storm over Dismas

Begin the adventure as follows:

Recently your erstwhile comrade-in-arms, Crawface, deserted with a whole squadron. Your mission is to capture and return him for a bounty of 140 gold pieces. You only get half if he's dead.

You believe you've tracked them to a seedy tavern near the docks, The Five Maids. You watch the tavern from the shadows of a nearby alley under cover of night and a heavy rainstorm. As thunder rolls in the distance, you ponder you first move. What will you do?

Scene 1. The Five Maids

The Five Maids is a rough establishment, named after the five fair wenches that serve its watery ale. Here you'll find hardy sailors, weary old men and other misfits who come for the ask-no-questions atmosphere and cheap drink.

On the first floor are several small rooms for rent. Covered in sawdust and straw, they can be rented for 1 gp a night as long as you don't mind sharing a squalid floor with ten other rough sleepers.

The five maids themselves do everything: serve food and drinks, cook and clean, and throw rowdier elements out if needed.

Big Frey speaks for the five maids and serves behind the counter. This stout, young women has a thick head of unruly red hair and a temper to match.

The Innkeeper Lies Beneath (Secret) Whilst most patrons remember Old Ben, the innkeeper, they haven't seen him in a while. That's because the five maids, sick of his foul temper, murdered him one night and buried his body in the basement. For this reason, they don't let strangers down to the basement and are apt to change the subject if Old Ben comes up in conversation.

If the players wait to see what happens read the following:

You stand for a while in the cold, wet alleyway. Your boots fill with water and you begin to shiver, but your patience is rewarded. Eventually several figures leave the tavern and move to an alleyway nearby. You instantly recognise several of the party: it is your quarry, the deserters!

Move to scene 2.

If the players enter the tavern read the following:

As you open the double doors you are overwhelmed by the warm stench of unwashed human bodies and stale ale. The tavern is busy tonight and the bawdy music playing is drowned out by the murmurs of the dishevelled and seedy patrons, chatting, gambling and drinking amongst themselves.

The deserters are spread out across the room amidst the patrons.

Players must make a passive perception check versus a DC 15 to spot the deserters amidst the crowd.

If the first players look out of place i.e. wealthy, fey (dwarf of elf) etc., they lose surprise. Read the following:

As you step past the bar the inn suddenly falls silent. Heads turn and red-rimmed eyes glare at you. Slowly, the low murmur returns, the bard in the corner continues to play and every turns back to what they were doing.

The deserters immediately realise they've been found and start to slowly make their way to the exit.

Allow the players an additional passive perception check versus a DC 10 to spot the deserters trying to sneak past player character through the crowd.

If the players ask Big Frey about a man with a scar on his face and succeed a Charisma (Intimidation or Persuasion) check versus DC 15 (advantage with a 1 gp or more bribe, disadvantage if the players are out-of-place as above) she will claim Crawface was in earlier and identify one or two deserters in the tavern as being his comrades.

If the players confront the deserters they attack while the rest of the deserters try to escape to meet Crawface (scene 2): either through the door or through a window if the player's block escape. All non-finesse melee attacks are at disadvantage due to the cramped conditions of the tavern.

If the player's do nothing after ten minutes the deserters sneak through the crowd and organise a surprise attack on the players.

Scene 4. The Rivals

After the players have dispatched or retreated from the deserters and realise Crawface has escaped again, read the following:

Suddenly a burst of laughter echoes through the alleyway, followed by a slow clap. You all turn to see a woman sitting daintily off a nearby rooftop - as though she'd been enjoying the whole fight. She is dressed in leather, head-to-foot, with an impressive array knives, ropes and other, mysterious weapons and tools strapped on. Her head is wrapped in a hijab, and only her lined face is visible, showing the wear of a few years.

If the players ask who she is she replies:

Who am I? she says, with a slight accent. Someone who could take this bounty from right under your nose, that's who!

If the players ask what is so funny she replies:

What is funny? Why are you running around in the rain chasing after these fools? This is not how things are done in my land. Let me say, there are easier ways to retrieve your bounty.

If the players ask about her past she replies:

Never mind I. More importantly do you know were Crawface will be at dawn?

What she wants: Help defeating Crawface and a cut of the bounty.

What she has: She stole a note from Crawface which mentions where he's headed.

The message reads:

Meet at Tyrell's Tree at dawn tomorrow. Do not be late on pain of death. T.

She appears disdainful of the players. Let the players haggle with her. Intimidation checks are made at a disadvantage due to her Fearless nature.

If they kill her they find the message on her body.

Scene 4. Rendezvous at Tyrell's Tree

Tyrell's Tree grows in the middle of Baalgard's market square. A single body swings gently from the bare branches. About it crows peck at the flesh, a grisly breakfast for the birds. It turns out the tree is regularly used for hangings. When you arrive it is early in the morn and the shops, carts and stands huddling nearby are deserted. The storm has abated and all is silent except for the cawing of crows.

Tyrell's Tree

Named after the legendary judge and priest who personally hanged his defendants. The daily hangings a the Tree are popular sport for the city folk who assemble in rowdy droves to cheer as criminals swing from the rope.

Players may take a short rest before this scene begins.

Ask the players what their tactic is. They have plenty of time to prepare so they can be hiding anywhere.

Appendix 1: Factions

The Revelatorium

Major secret faction (arcane, neutral evil)

Revelatorium are an ancient cabal of wizards who can trace their lineage back in an unbroken line, master to apprentice, to the very first magi.

Lyrian Bank

The Templars

Major secret faction (neutral evil)

Witches, magi, spirits: most sane folk consider them superstitious legends. Fortunately, the templars are crazy enough to still believe these creatures exist, gone into hiding in secret conspiracies. It is the templars ancient mission to hunt down and eradicate all magic in the world, lest it cause the Witch Kings to return again.

The Unseen School

Minor secret faction (neutral evil)

An age of new ideas needs a new magic. This offshoot of the Revelatorium no longer believes in return to the Old Empire.
