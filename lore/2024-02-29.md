> So we follow our players as they travail the streets of twilit Dismas, in a carriage stolen, carrying witches (counting two or perhaps three), one afflicted (poisoned or worse), one witchfinder, anchorites (perhaps as many as two), at least one carcass, and various others who betray classification.
>
> As directed by curious Sister Naive, or is that Lady Yenshaw, or whomever she might truly be.
>
> Our party pass beneath Hook Gate, a vast gatehouse that stands astride the Dismas streets, guarding the ghosts of walls long gone.
>
> The carriage make its way in stops and starts down the wide thoroughfare known to the local as the "Sods," in that colourful vernacular inimitable to the city.
>
> Beyond the remit of Dismas norms we find ourselves amongst sea travellers. About parade an endless, swirling stream of merchants, sailors, palanquins, pedlars, minstrels, tumblers, catamites, brahmins, preachers and so forth and so on. Now two figures alike, a hundred languages in the air.
>
> Yet, even for this sight they do not stop, wantonly turning into sullen backstreets and directed to halt outside a great town house.
>
> What lies within? Surely trouble. Can our players acquit themselves without harm. We shall survey...
