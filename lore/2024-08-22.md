> As our noblest audience assembles at their seats, we are best reminded of the destitution and dissolution of our fair city. Crowds of beggars gather ever more, on filthy streets. Market stalls stand bare, their wares as rotten as an old hag's teeth.
>
> Slowly, the city frays.
>
> An old maid gibbers nonsense about how candle nor wick will alight in her house.
>
> Whole families' flesh turns green, beneath the glare of balestar.
>
> A small child weeps as she is put abed, fearful that her shadow will go wandering again that night.
>
> The houses of Old Smell stand empty.
>
> Yet, decay is as much within as without. Hope snuffed out by despair, as though hope itself will not alight in our city.
>
> Of the victims, some we know well. Anoster, purveyor of books, once of the Temple of the Father, pickles his organs all in the name of pickling his brain.
>
> Yet, all is not lost. A friend offers him a shoulder and kind word...
